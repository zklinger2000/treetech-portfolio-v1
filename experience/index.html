A little history

My first "real" job was at an internet startup testing web sites, clicking buttons and banner ads. Later, I worked as a black box tester for video games. Believing I had more to offer, I became an instructor at a small art college teaching animation. Within a year, teaching students taught me one thing: I'd rather be working on projects.

After a few years of hard work, I had my name in the credits of movies, games and cartoons doing everything from animating superheroes to compositing spaceships over green screens.

Sounds great, but, I always regretted not learning to write code. No matter what I was doing, I kept coming to the realization that I could be "doing this better". I often knew exactly what I wanted done, in a sort of primal, pseudocode way, but could never stop working long enough to sit down and learn, really learn, how to write code and build the things I could imagine.

Not so long ago I committed myself to take the next step in this saga. First, I went back to school and took all the Computer Science courses my community college offered, all in C++, ending up with a 3.75 GPA. After that, like so many others, I set off on my own for a while, eventually finding "Web Developer" to be the job description I was working towards. Prepending "Full Stack" to that title being the next logical move.
